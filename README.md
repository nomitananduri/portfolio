## Loan Default Risk Analysis
**Tools:** Tableau

Analyzed a 255,000-record loan dataset to identify which borrower and loan characteristics most strongly predict default risk. Using Tableau, I tested hypotheses around credit score, income, interest rate, employment status, and education level, then built an interactive dashboard to visualize the findings.

**Key finding:** Borrowers with both low credit scores and low income defaulted at rates exceeding 30% — compared to under 10% for high credit/high income borrowers, a 5x difference. The analysis also showed that combining risk factors (rather than looking at any single variable) gives a much stronger picture of true default risk, leading to a recommendation that lenders evaluate borrowers holistically rather than on one metric alone.

## Into The Real World — UX Research
**Tools:** Figma, Google Surveys

Conceived and led a 4-person team through a full UX research process to design a financial literacy app aimed at helping high schoolers navigate real-world money and life skills. As project lead, I directed a 29-responder survey and 4 user interviews to understand where young people feel most unprepared for adulthood.

**Key finding:** 50% of survey respondents said they didn't feel ready for adulthood after graduating high school, and financial literacy was consistently cited as a major gap left by the traditional education system. These insights shaped the app's core features, information architecture, and prototype design, which underwent multiple rounds of usability testing and iteration before reaching a high-fidelity Figma prototype.
